The Transformative Power of Simple Questions

When trying to change team or an individual’s behaviour, use simple, distilled questions.

A well-crafted question will communicate the desired change, and provide focus (and some healthy pressure), causing the recipient(s) to re-evaluate, re-work, and commit to their response.

This question preserves (and enhances) the recipient’s autonomy and responsibility.

In contrast, other tools, such as checklists and process flow diagrams, introduce rigidity, brittleness, maintenance overhead, and a sense of disempowerment.

Example questions

“What did you learn [about users, feature performance, subject area etc]?”

This emphasises the important of discovery, synthesis, and encourages open-mindedness and exploration.

“Are you happy with this?”

This encourages individual and collective responsibility, emphasising quality, ownership, and the opportunity for course correction.

“Have you tested for [speed/accessibility/etc.]?”

This highlights a specific, previously overlooked factor, and communicates importance.

Crafting simple questions

  • Understand the goals and challenges of the business/team/individual.
  • Identify the core issue.
  • Assess the current state and define the desired state — this gap forms the core of your question.
  • Draft a simple, clear, empathetic, and forward-looking, closed format question.
  • Test, refine, and pose your question.
  • Follow up with supportive actions.
