The Transformative Power of Simple Questions

When trying to change team or an individual’s behaviour, use simple, distilled questions.

A well-crafted question will communicate the desired change and provide pressure, causing the recipient to re-evaluate, re-work (if necessary), and commit to their response. Fortunately, this question preserves (or even enhances) the recipient’s autonomy and responsibility.

In contrast, other tools, such as checklists and process flow diagrams, introduce rigidity, brittleness, maintenance overhead, and a sense of disempowerment.

Examples

  • "Are you happy with this?" - This question emphasises quality, ownership, and the opportunity for course correction.
  • ”Are you happy to release this?” - This encourages individual and collective responsibility for quality and user satisfaction.
  • “Have you tested for accessibility” - This question highlights a specific, previously overlooked factor.

Crafting a simple question

  • Understand the goals and challenges of the business/team/individual.
  • Identify the core issue.
  • Assess the current state and define the desired state—this gap forms the core of your question.
  • Draft a simple, clear, empathetic, and forward-looking question in a closed format.
  • Test, refine, and pose your question.
  • Follow up with supportive actions.
